Executive Thesis

Course pacing at Lowland is remarkably consistent — and consistently problematic. Average inter-course gaps hold steady at 22–24 minutes across all years, all volume levels, and all seasons. Over half of all multi-course tables (53–57%) experience 30+ minute waits between courses, and this long-wait percentage is the pacing metric most connected to guest perception (r = -0.30 vs recommend scores). Meanwhile, 9% of guests explicitly cite wait times in their surveys, rating service 0.5 points lower and recommend 1.0 point lower than average. The pacing issue is structural — it persists across all servers, all days, and all volume levels. Reducing the long-wait percentage is the highest-ROI operational improvement available.

Note: October 2024 originally showed a 51-min avg gap due to a single data artifact (held-open tab with 872-min “gap”). All figures in this report use corrected data — Oct 2024’s actual avg gap was 22.8 min.

5 Recommendations
Immediate — 0-30 Days

Kitchen Workflow Audit

  • Adopt Long Wait % as the primary pacing KPI — not average gap time
  • Audit kitchen expo workflow to identify why 55% of tables hit 30+ min gaps
  • Implement visual course timer on KDS flagging tables approaching 20 minutes
Short-term — 30-90 Days

Pacing Improvements

  • Target P90 course gap below 35 minutes
  • Pilot “next course alert” system between FOH and kitchen
  • Establish weekly pacing reports for management review
Medium-term — 90-180 Days

Structural Changes

  • Reduce long-wait percentage below 40%
  • Investigate May 2025 elevated gap (34.8 min) for possible data/operational issue
  • Establish 20-minute course pacing SLA with server communication protocol
