Hour of Day

KEY TAKEAWAY
The core 5PM–8PM dinner window still drives the business, but it's compressing. 6PM held almost perfectly ($218.5K → $216.6K, basically flat), which means prime-time demand is intact. The damage is on the edges: 5PM dropped 21% ($185K → $146K) — early diners are evaporating — and 9PM dropped 33% ($68K → $45K), signaling that late seatings are drying up. This "peak compression" means the restaurant is doing nearly the same volume but cramming it into a tighter window, which strains the kitchen during peak and leaves dead air before and after. The 11AM–1PM brunch band is also noticeably thinner, consistent with the 30% brunch order decline.

Day of Week

KEY TAKEAWAY

Thursday followed the same pattern (-20.5%, check down $12). Sunday was the biggest shock at -28.4%, driven by a 27.6% cover drop (2,230 → 1,614) — this is likely brunch weakness compounding dinner erosion. Meanwhile, Friday and Saturday average checks actually increased ($157→$167 and $157→$164 respectively), indicating weekend guests are trading up. Monday was the lone positive (+2.4%). This suggests the restaurant is losing its "midweek regular" base — the lower-check, higher-frequency diners — while special-occasion and weekend traffic remains strong.

Outdoor tables (garden, sidewalk, streetery) are only bookable April through October. During the 5 winter months (November-March), any shift that relies on outdoor tables for its online inventory loses those seats entirely.
